### Food Essentials
- [ ] **Spice packets** (Taco, Fajita, Italian) - foundation, no individual spices needed yet
- [ ] **Bouillon cubes** (chicken) - instant broth, no pre-boiling needed
- [ ] **Frozen pepper/onion mix** - no chopping, always ready
- [ ] **Canned green chilis** - secret weapon for heat and flavor
- [ ] **Lemon/lime juice** (bottled is fine) - essential acid for brightness
- [ ] **Oil** (avocado/canola/olive) - cooking fat, searing, drizzling
- [ ] **Rice** (Basmati/Jasmine/Long Grain White) - staple grain, pick one
- [ ] **Canned beans** (black, kidney, white) - protein/fiber, always ready

### Kitchen Tools (Minimum)
- [ ] **Wooden spatula** - won't scratch pressure cooker, essential for scraping
- [ ] **Trivet** (or foil for trivet trick) - keeps protein elevated, prevents burning
- [ ] **Salt/Pepper grinder** (McCormick makes an easy-to-find, inexpensive option) - **Quick flavor win + makes you feel like a chef!** Fresh cracked pepper is a game-changer.
- [ ] **Measuring cups/spoons** - accuracy matters for liquid ratios
- [ ] **Meal-sized reusable containers** (3-4) - meal prep essential

### Meal Prep Supplies
- [ ] **Freezer zip lock bags** (quart/gallon sizes) - portion freezing, burrito prep
- [ ] **Permanent marker** - label frozen items with date (prevents mystery food)

## ðŸŽ¯ Tier 2: Build Up To (Add As You Go)

### Individual Spices (Replace Packets Gradually)
- [ ] **Garlic powder** - most versatile spice
- [ ] **Onion powder** - base flavor, works everywhere
- [ ] **Oregano** - Mediterranean/Italian profiles (the loud, bold relative)
- [ ] **Thyme** - **The quiet, sophisticated hero.** Best herb for chicken and lemon. Makes dishes taste "expensive." The missing link.
- [ ] **Smoked Paprika** - **Essential upgrade.** Regular paprika adds little - smoked paprika is a flavor powerhouse.
- [ ] **Cumin** - Tex-Mex/Mexican, warm earthy flavor
- [ ] **Chili powder** - heat and color
- [ ] **Black pepper** (whole peppercorns) - upgrade your grinder
- [ ] **Salt** (kosher + table) - kosher for cooking, table for finishing

### Additional Acids
- [ ] **Apple cider vinegar** - versatile acid, great for many profiles
- [ ] **Red wine vinegar** - Mediterranean/Italian dishes
- [ ] **Balsamic vinegar** - Italian/Mediterranean, adds sweetness

### Umami Sources
- [ ] **Worcestershire sauce** - depth and umami, works in many dishes
- [ ] **Soy sauce** - Asian profiles, umami boost

### Protein Options
- [ ] **Chicken thighs** (buy in bulk, freeze in portions) - most forgiving cut
- [ ] **Canned beans variety pack** - different flavors for different recipes

## ðŸ’¡ Bachelor's Wisdom

### Start Simple
**Tier 1 gets you cooking immediately.** Don't overwhelm yourself. You can make amazing food with just Tier 1 items.

### Build Gradually
**As you use spice packets, read the ingredients.** Buy those individual spices next time. You'll naturally build your collection.

### Meal Prep = Freedom
**Having containers and bags ready means you'll actually do meal prep.** No more "I'll do it later" - you're set up for success.

### Tools Matter
- **Wooden spatula** is $5 and prevents scratching your $100 pressure cooker. Worth it.
- **Salt/Pepper grinder** is $8-12 and makes you feel like a chef. Plus, fresh cracked pepper is a flavor game-changer. This is a psychological win AND a flavor win.

### The Grinder Advantage
**Why the salt/pepper grinder is Tier 1:**
- **Inexpensive:** McCormick makes easy-to-find options at most grocery stores
- **Quick win:** Fresh cracked pepper elevates any dish instantly
- **Chef confidence:** Using a grinder makes you feel more professional
- **Versatility:** Works for finishing any protein, rice, or bowl
- **No skill required:** Just twist and grind - immediate upgrade

### Print This
**Keep this list in your wallet/phone.** Check it before grocery shopping. Having a list prevents "what do I need?" moments in the store.
