# Quick Reference Glossary

## Abbreviations

- **HP** = High Pressure
- **LP** = Low Pressure
- **NR** = Natural Release
- **QR** = Quick Release
- **10m NR** = 10 minutes natural release, then quick release
- **PIP** = Pot-in-Pot method
- **Sauté** = Sauté mode (browning/searing)

## Cooking Terms

- **Deglaze:** Adding liquid to scrape up browned bits after searing
- **Fond:** The browned bits stuck to the bottom of the pot (flavor gold)
- **Maillard Reaction:** The browning/searing process that creates deep flavors
- **Natural Release:** Letting pressure drop naturally (10-15 minutes)
- **Quick Release:** Manually releasing pressure immediately
- **Sear:** Browning the surface of protein before pressure cooking

## Flavor Terms

- **Acid:** Bright, tangy flavors (lemon, vinegar) that cut through fat
- **Umami:** Savory, meaty depth (Worcestershire, soy sauce, mushrooms)
- **Base Spices:** Universal seasonings (garlic powder, onion powder, oregano)
- **Late-Stage Drizzle:** Adding acids/fresh herbs after cooking

## Equipment

- **Trivet:** Metal rack that elevates food in pot (for PIP method)
- **Steamer Basket:** Alternative to trivet for PIP method
- **Silicone Container:** Oven-safe container for PIP rice cooking
