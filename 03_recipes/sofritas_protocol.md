## Instructions

### Phase 1: Prep (2 minutes)
1. **Drain + Quick Press:** Drain tofu. Wrap in paper towels and press for **1 minute** (hands or a plate). You‚Äôre not ‚Äúfully pressing‚Äù it‚Äîjust removing surface water.
2. **Bachelor Crumble:** Crumble the tofu into rough ‚Äúground meat‚Äù bits with your hands (right into a bowl or plate). Perfect cubes are not the goal.

### Phase 2: Saut√©-Dry (3-4 minutes)
**What You‚Äôre Doing:** Drying + browning tofu a little so it doesn‚Äôt taste wet/spongy.

1. Press **Saut√©**. Add **1 tbsp oil**.
2. Add the crumbled tofu. Sprinkle **1 tsp salt** over it.
3. Cook **3-4 minutes**, stirring occasionally, until it looks drier and you see some light browning.

### Phase 3: Deglaze (30 seconds)
1. Pour in **1/2 cup water**.
2. Scrape the bottom firmly with a wooden spatula until nothing is stuck. (This is how you avoid **BURN**.)

### Phase 4: Dump + Pressure Braise (10 minutes HP, QR)
1. **Dump:** Add **tomatoes**, **chipotle in adobo** (to your heat level), **1 tsp cumin**, and **1 tsp garlic powder**. Stir.
2. **Pressure Cook:** High Pressure **10 minutes**.
3. **Quick Release:** Vent immediately (QR).

### Phase 5: Finish (0-2 minutes)
1. If it looks watery, press **Saut√©** for **1-2 minutes** to boil off excess and thicken.
2. Taste and adjust:
   - More salt if it tastes flat
   - More adobo sauce if it needs heat/smoke
3. Optional: squeeze **lime juice** over the top after cooking.
