**🧱 New Lego Block: Jarred-Sauce Fusion + Long Reduction + "Trace" Spicing**

**📚 Building on the Foundation** — This recipe does **not** use the pressure cooker; it’s a **stovetop concentrate** you can deploy on bowls, eggs, sandwiches, and anything that wants ketchup-adjacent heat. For **umami and spice logic**, see `01_fundamentals/spices_and_flavor.md`. For **storage habits**, see `02_techniques/meal_prep_storage.md`.

**What You’re Learning Here:**
- **Bloom-then-build:** soften shallots, then wake a tiny amount of dry spice at the end of the sauté
- **Reduction as “salt management”:** no added salt; sodium concentrates as water leaves
- **Rustic body vs. smooth:** skipping the sieve on purpose (optional smooth path in Version History)

**Use This Technique In:** Any “bottle sauce + salsa + heat” fusion when you want depth without starting from raw tomatoes.

**📦 Pantry Impact:** MEDIUM — commits you to **Rao’s Pizza Sauce**, **Mateo’s Cantina salsa**, and **Frank’s RedHot** as the backbone; everything else is standard fridge/dry goods.

## Ingredients

### The Backbone (Jarred)
- [ ] **2 cups (16 oz)** Rao’s Pizza Sauce
- [ ] **1 cup (8 oz)** Mateo’s Salsa Cantina Style
- [ ] **½ cup (4 oz)** Frank’s RedHot Original

### Aromatics & Bridges
- [ ] **4–5 medium shallots** — sliced into thin coins
- [ ] **2 tbsp** lime juice *(fresh or bottled)*
- [ ] **2 tbsp** pickle juice *(from a dill pickle jar is fine)*

### The “Mystery” Spice (Keep It Subtle)
- [ ] **¼ tsp** garam masala — *“beta” level so it reads as depth, not “curry”*

### Fat for the Pan
- [ ] **1–2 tbsp** neutral oil *or* butter — *for sautéing shallots*

---

## Execution

### Phase 1: Aromatic Bloom
1. Heat oil/butter in a **medium saucepan** (or deep skillet) over **low to medium-low**.
2. Add **shallot coins**. Cook until **soft and translucent** with a little color—**ribbons**, not crispy fries.
3. For the last **~45 seconds**, add **¼ tsp garam masala** and stir constantly so it **blooms** without burning.

### Phase 2: Component Fusion
1. Add **Rao’s**, **Mateo’s**, **Frank’s**, **lime juice**, and **pickle juice**.
2. Stir until unified, scraping the bottom once everything is wet.

### Phase 3: Deep Reduction (The “Ketchup Zone”)
1. Bring to a gentle bubble, then reduce heat to **low / low-medium** to hold a **steady simmer**.
2. Simmer **30–50 minutes**, stirring occasionally—**more often** as it thickens (stick/burn risk rises at the end).
3. **Target consistency:** between a **thick glaze** and **pourable ketchup**.
4. **Canyon test:** drag a spoon through the center; the trail should **hold for a split second**, then **slowly** close.
5. **Volume:** expect roughly **30–40%** reduction from where you started (evaporation concentrates salt and flavor).

### Phase 4: Integrated Finish (No-Sieve v2.0)
1. Cool slightly, then bottle.
2. **v2.0 texture rule:** do **not** sieve. The long simmer should turn shallots into **soft, jammy bits**—tactile, not crunchy-chunky.

## 🧪 Chemistry Notes (Bachelor Logic)

- **Trace garam masala:** at **¼ tsp** in this volume, warm spice reads as **extra tomato/savory depth** (“natural MSG” vibe) rather than a defined Indian profile—keep it there unless you’re intentionally steering the flavor.
- **Inherited salt:** **Frank’s** + **Rao’s** carry the sodium; reduction **concentrates** it—another reason to avoid adding salt until you’ve tasted **cold** sauce later.
- **Acid bridges:** lime + pickle juice adds **brightness** and a **fermented/salty** note without leaning only on vinegar heat from Frank’s.
- **Why no sieve in v2.0:** body stays **rustic**; long cook time is doing texture work on the shallots.

## ⚠️ Troubleshooting

**For universal troubleshooting:** See `04_reference/universal_troubleshooting.md`

### Recipe-Specific Issues

- **Burning on the bottom?** Lower heat, use a heavier pan, and switch to **more frequent** stirring earlier; add a **splash of water** only if you’re genuinely scorching (it dilutes—prefer prevention).
- **Too salty after reduction?** Next time shorten simmer **or** cut Frank’s slightly; you can also dilute **this** batch with a little extra **Rao’s** and re-reduce gently (taste often).
- **Too thin at 30 minutes?** Keep going—**wide pan = faster** evaporation; **lid off** always for this recipe.
- **Too thick / candy-like?** You overshot; next time pull earlier or lower heat; you can rescue with tiny splashes of **water or Rao’s** and stir off heat.
