## Pantry Building Strategy

### Tier System for New Ingredients

#### Tier A: Versatile & Worth It (Buy These)
**These ingredients have multiple uses and won't waste:**

1. **Honey** ($5-8, lasts 1+ year)
   - Used in: Honey Garlic, Teriyaki
   - Other uses: Tea, toast, other recipes, natural sweetener
   - **Verdict:** BUY - Versatile and long-lasting

2. **Rice Vinegar** ($3-5, lasts 1+ year)
   - Used in: Teriyaki, can enhance other Asian dishes
   - Other uses: Salad dressings, pickling, other Asian recipes
   - **Verdict:** BUY - Versatile for Asian cooking

3. **Sesame Oil** ($4-6, lasts 6+ months)
   - Used in: Gochujang, can enhance Teriyaki
   - Other uses: Finishing oil for Asian dishes, salad dressings
   - **Verdict:** BUY - Versatile finishing oil

#### Tier B: Single-Use but Cheap (Buy When Making That Recipe)
**These are cheap enough to buy when needed:**

1. **Jarred Salsa Verde** ($3-4, lasts 2-3 weeks in fridge)
   - Only used in: Pulled Chicken Taco
   - **Strategy:** Buy when making recipe, use within 2 weeks
   - **Verdict:** BUY WHEN NEEDED - Cheap, but single-use

2. **Store-bought Tzatziki** ($4-6, lasts 1 week in fridge)
   - Only used in: Greek Lemon
   - **Strategy:** Buy when making recipe, use within week
   - **Verdict:** BUY WHEN NEEDED - Cheap, but single-use

#### Tier C: Single-Use & Expensive (Substitute or Skip)
**These are expensive and single-use - provide substitutions:**

1. **Mirin/Rice Wine** ($5-8, lasts 6+ months)
   - Only used in: Teriyaki
   - **Substitution:** White wine + sugar, OR skip entirely
   - **Verdict:** SUBSTITUTE - Not worth buying for one recipe

2. **Gochujang Paste** ($5-8, lasts 6+ months)
   - Only used in: Gochujang recipe
   - **Substitution:** Sriracha + honey + soy sauce (approximation)
   - **Verdict:** SUBSTITUTE OR SKIP - Expensive for single use

3. **Dried Dill** ($3-5, lasts 1+ year)
   - Only used in: Greek Lemon
   - **Substitution:** Skip, or use fresh dill if available
   - **Verdict:** SUBSTITUTE - Single-use spice

4. **Dried Mint** ($3-5, lasts 1+ year)
   - Only used in: Greek Lemon
   - **Substitution:** Skip, or use fresh mint if available
   - **Verdict:** SUBSTITUTE - Single-use spice

## Substitution Strategies

### For Mirin (Teriyaki Recipe)
**Option 1: White Wine + Sugar**
- 1 tbsp mirin = 1 tbsp white wine + 1/2 tsp sugar
- **Pro:** Uses common ingredient (white wine)
- **Con:** Slightly different flavor

**Option 2: Skip Mirin**
- Recipe still works without it
- **Pro:** No extra purchase
- **Con:** Slightly less authentic flavor

**Option 3: Apple Juice + Rice Vinegar**
- 1 tbsp mirin = 1 tbsp apple juice + 1 tsp rice vinegar
- **Pro:** Uses pantry staples
- **Con:** Different flavor profile

### For Gochujang (Gochujang Recipe)
**Option 1: Sriracha + Honey + Soy Sauce**
- 1 tbsp gochujang ≈ 1 tsp sriracha + 1 tsp honey + 1 tsp soy sauce
- **Pro:** Uses pantry staples
- **Con:** Less complex flavor (missing fermented depth)

**Option 2: Skip Recipe**
- If gochujang is hard to find or expensive
- **Pro:** No waste
- **Con:** Missing trendy recipe

**Option 3: Buy Small Jar**
- Some stores sell small jars ($3-4)
- **Pro:** Try it, minimal investment
- **Con:** Still single-use if you don't love it

### For Dried Dill & Mint (Greek Lemon Recipe)
**Option 1: Skip Spices**
- Recipe works with just oregano + lemon
- **Pro:** No extra purchase
- **Con:** Less "Greek" flavor

**Option 2: Fresh Herbs (If Available)**
- Use fresh dill/mint if you see it
- **Pro:** Better flavor
- **Con:** Requires shopping trip

**Option 3: Italian Seasoning Blend**
- Many blends include dill/mint
- **Pro:** Versatile blend
- **Con:** Less precise flavor
