Your intuition is correct: **Pressure cookers are closed systems.** Unlike a stovetop, almost zero liquid evaporates during the pressurized phase. In fact, chicken thighs actually *release* about 1/4 cup of juice as they cook.

If you start with 3 cups of broth and only use 1.5 cups of rice, you will likely end up with **"Burrito Soup"** or mushy rice.

# 03_Recipes: Chipotle-Style Burrito Bowl (v2.0)
**Profile:** Tex-Mex | **Hardware:** Trivet Required | **Method:** Sequential One-Pot

- [ ] **1.5 cups** White Basmati Rice (Rinsed thoroughly)
- [ ] **1.75 cups** Chicken Broth (The "Logic" constant)
- [ ] **1 can** Black Beans (Drained/Rinsed)
- [ ] **Seasoning:** 1 Taco Packet OR (2tsp Cumin, 1tsp Chili Powder, 1tsp Garlic Powder, 1tsp Salt)
- [ ] **The Drizzle:** 2 Limes (Fresh) + Fresh Cracked Pepper

---

## ‚ö° Phase 1: The Protein Cook
1.  **Pour 1.75 cups broth** into the pot. Stir in your **Seasonings**.
2.  **Insert Trivet.** Place chicken thighs on top of the trivet (keeping them out of the broth).
3.  **Pressure Cook:** 
    *   **Thawed:** 8 mins HP.
    *   **Frozen:** 12 mins HP.
4.  **Release:** 10 mins Natural Release (NR), then vent.
5.  **The Rest:** Remove chicken to a plate. **Drizzle immediately with the juice of 1 lime.** Cover with foil.

---

## üçö Phase 2: The Flavor-Wash Rice
1.  **Check Liquid:** You should still see roughly the same amount of broth. If it looks very low, add a splash more to hit the 1.5-cup mark (eyeball it to the rice level).
2.  **Add Rice:** Pour **1.5 cups rinsed Basmati** directly into the flavored broth. 
3.  **Add Beans:** Dump the beans on top of the rice (do not stir deep, just a light pat down).
4.  **Pressure Cook:** 6 mins HP.
5.  **Release:** 10 mins NR. This is the "Starch Set" phase. **Do not skip.**

---

## üçã Phase 3: The Bachelor Finish
1.  **Shred:** While rice finishes, shred or slice your rested chicken. 
2.  **Combine:** Open the pot, fluff the rice/bean mix. 
3.  **The Final Drizzle:** Add the sliced chicken back in or serve on top. Hit the whole bowl with the **second lime** and **cracked pepper**.
4.  **Chemistry Note:** The lime cuts the chicken fat; the pepper adds "high notes" that pressure cooking usually kills.
